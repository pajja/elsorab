Laila Sorabji (El Sorab) is a multimedia artist and tattooist whose practice is grounded in Anthropology, and specifically the politics of the body. Working directly on skin, she treats the human body as both site and subject - mapping relationships between the self, natural world, and systems of power that humans find themselves entangled within. Inflected with subtle, satirical humour, her work claims bodily marking as an act of agency and becoming.

She explores and cultivates a relationship between the human body and the natural world. Her practice involves connecting the contours of the body with designs influenced by textures and geometries appearing in nature. Free-handing designs allows her to prioritise the client; putting the body and its form first. Her work connects people with nature and its intricacies, for example, by adding woodgrain textures to skin, the line between tree and person blurs in a beautiful way.
